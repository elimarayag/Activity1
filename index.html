    My works as a visual artist

    Awake: trying to stay awake and remember every real thing that happened.

    I tried drowning my emotions through drinking and smoking, but nothing worked. Nothing made me feel better; it all made me feel worse. I guess that's the thing about being sick—nothing you do will ever make it go away. It feels like you're stuck in a cycle, desperately searching for an escape that doesn't exist. And the more you try, the deeper you sink into the darkness.

    Echohead: a head full of echoes, repeating thoughts, or voices that refuse to fade away

    Underneath: what lives inside. The real part, waiting, watching, rising.

    Spillpoint: not a meltdown from rage but one from exhaustion. The kind you don't come back from easily.

    Snapped: he used to fake it. now it’s real and deadly.

    A figure dissolves into the background, his form fading with each tear that falls. The tears don’t just express sorrow—they transform him, blending his being into the very fabric behind him. Slowly, he becomes part of the silence he once stood against.

    A series of eyes stare out from the canvas, each one a window into a different storm—rage, sorrow, joy, fear, and hope flicker in shifting reflections. No words are spoken, yet the eyes scream, whisper, ache, and shine, capturing the raw chaos of being human.

    A lone skull rests in the center, not lifeless but hauntingly expressive. Cracks and shadows form subtle shapes—echoes of laughter, pain, and longing—as if the remnants of emotion still cling to the bone. It is death, yes, but not silent—it's memory carved in calcium.
